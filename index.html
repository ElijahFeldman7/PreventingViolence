My Action Plan By Elijah Feldman

Choosing a path of non-violence and strength.

Part 1: My Current Activities and Use of Time

Now that school is out, I spend most of my time on my passions like web development and personal research.

I also love to go to the pool, and every morning go to swim practice. I play with friends and swim competitively.

Overall, I think I have a good balance. The time I need to be careful with is on the weekends when there is less structure. That's when poor decisions could be made, so I need to have a plan.

Part 2: Personal Ways to Deal with Peer Pressure

I've thought about what I would do if my friends wanted to get into a fight or do something violent. It's a scary thought, but I have a plan that makes me feel more in control.

  • I Will Use Humor to Deflect: If someone suggests doing something aggressive, I'd try to make a joke to change the mood. This can break the tension in the room.
  • I Will Suggest Something Else: If one of my friends suggests and tries to peer pressure me into performing malicious activities, I could just suggest something else such as playing basketball
  • I Have an Excuse Ready: My parents are pretty strict about where I am. I will use that to my advantage. Saying "I have to get home, my mom will get mad at me" will be an excuse to get me out of certain scenarios
  • I Will Walk Away: If things get serious, I won't hesitate to just leave. My safety is more important than what anyone thinks of me.

Part 3: My Protective Factors

I know I have strengths and supports in my life that protect me. These are the things that keep me grounded and focused on a good future.

  • My Family: This is my biggest protective factor. My parents and I have a good relationship. We have dinner together almost every night and we talk about what's going on. They trust me, and I don't want to break that trust.
  • My Future Goals: I want to build products that help people. I know that getting involved in violence or substance abuse could ruin that goal. Keeping my goals in mind helps me make better choices.
  • Good Friends: Most of my friends are like me. They are focused on school and sports. We support each other in making good decisions.

Part 4: My Plan and Trusted Adults

Having a plan is just an idea until you know who you can rely on for help. I have a few trusted adults I know I can go to for anything.

My Trusted Adults:

  • My Dad
  • My Mom
  • My Swim Coaches

My Concrete Action Plan:

If I am ever in a situation where I feel pressured, unsafe, or need to get out, here is my step-by-step plan:

  • Step 1: I will use one of my excuses, like needing to get home, to try and leave calmly.
  • Step 2: If I can't leave easily, I will just send a text to my parents.
  • Step 3: I will immediately go to a safe, public place if possible (like a well-lit store) and wait for my ride.
  • Step 4: I will tell my trusted adult what happened once I am safe.
